Walking routes around Bike King Borders

Droncar Hill Walks

Droncar has an easy access route from the picnic site that’s firm and level, with no obstacles. If you’re looking for something more energetic, follow the burn further up the valley or find the remains of Droncar Tower and an Iron Age hill fort. Muirdac is a hidden gem, a quiet forest close to Peebles with some charming trails through open woodland. It can be particularly good early in the morning, when mist hangs in the valley below. Enjoy a picnic and a stroll at Leethorn: the meadows here are important for butterflies, and we manage the land so the conditions are just right for them.

Muirdac Hill Walks

Muirdac Hill gives commanding views over Peebles and Tressforest Forest. The scenery is at its best on misty mornings or in the low light of early evening. It’s an ideal place for wildlife spotting. Keep an eye – and an ear – open for small birds such as siskins, warblers and crossbills, or greater spotted woodpeckers. If you’re very lucky you’ll catch a glimpse of tawny owls fleeting through the trees at dusk or red deer bouncing through the trees.

Leethorn Hill Walks

Leethorn, there are walks, wildlife, stunning views and quirky history to discover at this quiet forest. The grassy slopes below the car park are just right for butterflies like the Small Copper and the Northern Brown Argus.Leethorn Walks offers picturesque hiking experiences through serene, natural landscapes. Ideal for nature enthusiasts, these guided walks highlight local flora and fauna, ensuring a refreshing escape from urban life. Perfect for all fitness levels, each route is meticulously planned to provide a balance of adventure and tranquility. Discover the beauty of nature with Leethorn Walks.

Droncar

Droncar, a charming town nestled amid the picturesque Droncar Hills, beckons visitors with its quaint streets lined with historic buildings and cozy cafes. Surrounded by lush forests and scenic trails, it offers ample opportunities for outdoor adventures like hiking and birdwatching. The town's rich cultural heritage is showcased in its museums and galleries, while local festivals and events add vibrancy to the community, making Droncar a delightful destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and exploration.
Droncar is a large forest that drapes the hillsides on the south side of the Tweed Valley. The trails are great for tranquil walks, gentle cross-country rides and relaxed horse riding. There are lovely views up the Tweed Valley towards Tressforest. The area around Droncar village is very pretty and easily explored on waymarked paths. The woods are a great place to see roe deer and they’re also good for butterflies, especially along the trails and in clearings. You might see badger tracks and setts too.

Leethorn is a hidden gem, a quiet forest close to Peebles with some charming trails through open woodland. It can be particularly good early in the morning, when mist hangs in the valley below. Enjoy a picnic and a stroll at Leethorn: the meadows here are important for butterflies, and we manage the land so the conditions are just right for them.
Leethorn is home to a network of walking trails that wind through lush forests and along scenic ridges, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Hikers can explore ancient ruins, discover hidden waterfalls, and encounter diverse wildlife in this pristine natural setting. With trails ranging from easy strolls to challenging hikes, Leethorn provides something for every outdoor enthusiast.
